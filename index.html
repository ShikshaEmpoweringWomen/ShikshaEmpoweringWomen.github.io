Project Shiksha

About Us

Project SHIKSHA ‘empowering women’ stands to remove the myths associated with the women and aspires to provide them means and ways to protect themselves from all forms of wrongdoing and sidelining. Education is the cardinal and foremost tool by which women in underdeveloped areas can be empowered and taught to live life independently with respect.
Women have become an object of endurance in this era for they are forced to bear the wrath of ruthless, heinous acts that surround them. Every year contrary to the booming economic development across the globe, the condition of women is simply deteriorating that too at an unpleasant rate. Project SHIKSHA ‘empowering women’ was started with the aim to bring change in the life of women by teaching them and motivating them for better a future. For ages they have been considered as just the backbone of a family, now it’s time that they hold their heads high and embrace the world having plenty of opportunities for their empowerment.
Today, even the World Bank has acknowledged that there is no investment more effective for achieving overall development goals than educating girls.

Our Mission

Our mission is to reach out to as many women in rural areas as possible to educated them and to motivate them and in turn help them to become financially independent.

Our Vision

To work for uplifting the status of women in the society.
Create employment for women in rural areas to make them financially independent.
Create awareness about social and health issues related to women.

Our Journey So Far..

5th June, 2014

It was time that we started materialising our brainchild. The first step was to convince the parents of all the girls in our target villages. We decided to visit villages in Farrukhabad district of Uttar Pradesh. Our main aim was to make them realise the importance of education and how it can drastically affect the lives of all the girls we intended to educate.

1st July,2014

We started our classroom programme with two volunteers- Jenya Krivlop from Ukraine and Ildar Muzafraov from Moscow, Russia. Our main focus was on personality development and working on English speaking and writing skills of the girls. In the first class, we managed to get only six girls. But within a week, the count increased to 107. The girls were generally between 6-18 years of age.

16th July,2014

We organized a motivational seminar. In accordance with our expectations, 145 girls attended the seminar. We guided the girls and motivated them for a better future. We told them about the opportunities that the world holds and encouraged them to participate more and more in the society.

23rd July,2014

We organized a card-making and pot making competition. This event saw a participation of 68 girls. We then organized a cultural exchange seminar which had a series of competitions including singing, dancing, fashion show, etc. A total of 43 girls participated in the event and Aranza Guadarrama from Mexico was our Chief Guest. Everybody demonstrated their talents including our volunteers Jenya Krivlop and Ildar Muzafraov.
